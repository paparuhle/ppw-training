Chapter 1: Introduction to SaunaLife

What Is SaunaLife?

SaunaLife is a Scandinavian-designed sauna brand distributed by Bathing Brands out of Wheeling, Illinois. They design and manufacture complete sauna rooms — barrel saunas, cube saunas, garden cabins, indoor saunas, and even hot/cold immersion tubs. Think of them as the company that builds the room you sweat in, not the heater that makes it hot.

This is an important distinction you'll use every single day: SaunaLife makes the sauna body. The heater is sold separately. Every SaunaLife sauna ships without a heater. Your job is to pair the right sauna with the right heater from Harvia, HUUM, or Saunum.

Key Concept — Sauna Body vs. Heater: A sauna purchase has two parts: the room (the SaunaLife structure — walls, benches, door, roof) and the heater (sold separately from Harvia, HUUM, or Saunum). The customer's total investment = sauna body price + heater price ($2,000–$4,000) + electrician installation (varies by area — recommend they get a local quote).

Why Does PPW Carry SaunaLife?

SaunaLife gives us a complete sauna ecosystem under one brand — 30 models across 7 product series, covering every budget from $2,490 to $32,900. Here's why that matters for you as a sales advisor:

  • One brand, every customer: Whether someone wants a $3,990 cube for two people or a $24,900 pre-assembled flagship, SaunaLife has it. You don't need to juggle multiple sauna manufacturers.
  • Thermo-modified wood construction: SaunaLife uses thermally treated European spruce and aspen — wood that's been baked at extreme temperatures to become rot-resistant and weather-proof. This gives their saunas a 20+ year lifespan, far outlasting standard cedar competitors.
  • ERGO barrel diameters: While most competitors sell barrels at 67–72 inches in diameter, SaunaLife's E-Series starts at 81 inches and the EE-Series goes to 91 inches. That extra space is the difference between hunching over and sitting comfortably upright.
  • Heater flexibility: Because heaters are sold separately, customers can choose the right brand/tier for their budget — from reliable Harvia (Toyota) to design-forward HUUM (BMW) to premium Saunum (Bentley).

Chapter 2: Understanding Saunas (Foundations)

Before you can sell a sauna, you need to understand what one actually is and how the different types work. If you've never been in a sauna, think of it as a small, insulated wooden room designed to get very hot — typically 150–200°F. People sit inside for 15–30 minutes for relaxation, stress relief, muscle recovery, and overall wellness.

Key Terms You'll Use Every Day

Traditional Sauna (Finnish Sauna): A sauna heated by an electric or wood-burning heater with stones on top. You pour water on the hot stones to create steam (called löyly, pronounced "LOW-loo"). This is what SaunaLife saunas are designed for. It's different from an infrared sauna, which uses light panels instead of heated air.

Thermo-Modified Wood: Wood that's been heated to extreme temperatures in a special kiln with no oxygen. This permanently changes the wood's cell structure, making it resistant to rot, moisture, insects, and warping. Think of it like tempering steel — the heat treatment makes it fundamentally tougher. SaunaLife uses three types: Thermo-Spruce (exterior walls), Thermo-Aspen (benches — stays cool to touch), and Thermo-Pine (tubs).

ERGO Diameter: SaunaLife's proprietary barrel diameter. The E-Series uses an 81-inch diameter barrel (vs. competitors' 67–72 inches). The EE-Series goes up to 91 inches. A bigger diameter means more headroom — you can sit fully upright on the upper bench without ducking. Think of it like the difference between economy and business class legroom.

Stave: One of the long wooden planks that forms the barrel wall. Barrel saunas are built by fitting staves together side by side, then cinching them tight with stainless steel bands — like the planks of a wooden boat. Stave thickness determines insulation: standard E-Series staves are thinner, while GL-Series staves are 1.57 inches thick for maximum insulation.

kW (Kilowatt): The power rating of a sauna heater. Higher kW = more heating power. A small 2-person sauna might need 4.5kW; a large 8-person sauna needs 9–12kW. Think of it like engine horsepower — bigger room needs a bigger engine.

240V Circuit: The electrical service required for sauna heaters. This is the same type of circuit your clothes dryer or oven runs on. It requires a dedicated circuit and a licensed electrician to install. Every SaunaLife sauna heater runs on 240V.

Assembly — What Customers Need to Know

Almost every SaunaLife sauna is a DIY flat-pack kit. The one exception is the G6 ($24,900), which arrives fully pre-assembled. Here's how to set customer expectations:

  • Ships flat-packed: All parts, staves, bands, hardware arrive in crates on a freight truck.
  • 2 people minimum: Every model requires at least two adults for assembly.
  • Time range: 6 hours (small barrel) to 40 hours (G11 two-room cabin). See Chapter 6 for exact times per model.
  • Tools: Basic hand tools, drill, level. Larger models may need crane assistance.
  • Professional option: Assembly services available — get a quote from local installers. PPW's Peak Protection Concierge can help coordinate.
  • Heater separate: After sauna assembly, a licensed electrician installs the heater (typically 2–3 hours — recommend customers get a local quote).

Watch Out: Never tell a customer "it's easy to assemble." Assembly ranges from moderate to high difficulty. Always be upfront: "Budget a full weekend for assembly, or we can connect you with professional installers." Under-promising and over-delivering on assembly expectations prevents returns and bad reviews.

Installation Essentials

Foundation Requirements (All Outdoor Models)

  • Surface: Level, stable, load-bearing foundation (concrete pad, compacted gravel, or reinforced deck)
  • Height: Minimum 3 inches above surrounding ground to prevent water pooling
  • Clearance: 12 inches minimum on all sides for maintenance and airflow
  • Drainage: Remove soil underneath to prevent moisture/fungal growth; ensure water drains away
  • Heater ventilation: 3–4 feet clearance for heater ventilation (check specific heater requirements)
  • Access: Clear path for delivery truck (barrels: 500–1,500 lbs; cabins/cubes: 1,100–6,500 lbs)

Indoor Installation (X-Series)

  • Level, dry indoor floor (basement or dedicated room)
  • Adequate room ventilation or external venting recommended
  • 240V circuit within 15 feet of installation location
  • Waterproof flooring recommended under and around sauna
