Chapter 2: Understanding Sauna Heaters (Foundations)

Before we dive into specific Saunum models, let's make sure you understand the core concepts behind sauna heaters. If you already know this stuff, skim through — but don't skip the Saunum-specific concepts at the end of this chapter.

Key Terms You Need to Know

Key Definition — Kilowatt (kW): A unit of electrical power. In sauna heaters, kW tells you how much heating power the heater produces. Higher kW = more heat output = can heat a larger room. Think of it like horsepower in a car: a 5kW heater is a sedan, a 15kW heater is a truck.

Key Definition — Temperature Stratification: The natural phenomenon where hot air rises to the ceiling and cool air sinks to the floor, creating dramatic temperature layers inside a sauna. In a typical sauna, the ceiling might be 200°F while the floor is under 100°F — that's a 100°F difference in just 7 feet. This is the core problem Saunum solves.

Key Definition — Climate Equalizer: Saunum's patented system that actively blends the hot ceiling air with the cool floor air and redistributes it throughout the sauna room. This is the technology that makes Saunum unique — no other heater brand has anything like it.

Key Definition — Stone Capacity: The weight of sauna stones a heater can hold, measured in pounds (lbs) or kilograms (kg). More stones = more thermal mass = softer, more even heat and better steam production. Think of stones as a "thermal battery" — they store heat energy and release it gradually, especially when you throw water on them.

Key Definition — Incoloy Elements: A type of stainless steel alloy used in heating elements. Incoloy resists corrosion from water and steam much better than standard steel, which is critical in a sauna where you're constantly throwing water on hot stones. All Saunum heaters use Incoloy elements.

Key Definition — UL Certification (Underwriters Laboratories): An independent safety certification that's essentially required for electrical products in North America. A UL-listed heater has been tested and certified to meet strict safety standards. This is a significant differentiator — not all premium European sauna brands have UL certification for the US market.

Key Definition — Cubic Feet (cu. ft.): How sauna room size is measured. You calculate it by multiplying Length × Width × Height (all in feet). A typical home sauna might be 6' × 4' × 7' = 168 cu. ft. This number determines which heater size you need.

How an Electric Sauna Heater Works

An electric sauna heater is straightforward. Electricity flows through heating elements (the coils inside the heater), which get extremely hot. Sauna stones are piled on top of and around these elements. The stones absorb the heat and radiate it into the room. When you throw water on the hot stones, it instantly flashes into steam (called löyly — pronounced "LOW-lu" — the Finnish word for sauna steam).

The heater connects to a controller (a separate device mounted on the wall or accessed via an app) that lets you set the temperature, timer, and other settings.

How Saunum Is Different from Every Other Heater

Here's where Saunum breaks the mold. A conventional heater just generates heat and hope. Saunum adds three integrated systems:

  1. Telescopic Hot Air Intake — extends up near the ceiling and captures the hottest, most oxygen-depleted air
  2. Heat Equalizing Module — a whisper-quiet, 3-speed fan system inside the heater body that blends the hot ceiling air with cool, oxygen-rich floor air
  3. Blended Air Output — distributes the mixed air back into the room at a comfortable temperature with better oxygen content

As the blended air passes through the heater body, it also flows across the Himalayan salt tray and optional aromatherapy system, picking up salt ions and scent before being distributed into the room.

How to Frame This:

The core concept: Think of a conventional heater like opening a window in summer — hot air blasts in and you deal with it. Saunum is like central air conditioning — it actively manages temperature, airflow, and air quality throughout the entire space.

What the customer needs to understand: The Climate Equalizer isn't an accessory or add-on. It's built into the heater itself. Every Saunum heater is both a heater AND an air management system in one unit.

Wall-Mount vs. Floor-Standing

Saunum makes two form factors:

  • Wall-mounted (Air series) — hangs on the wall. Better for smaller saunas where floor space is limited. Uses a telescopic intake that extends up toward the ceiling.
  • Floor-standing (Air L series) — sits on the floor. Designed for larger, more intensively used saunas. Same telescopic system reaches up to capture ceiling air. "L" stands for "Large."

Both series use the exact same Climate Equalizer technology. The difference is size, power range, and stone capacity.

Watch Out: Do NOT make medical claims about Himalayan salt benefits. Saunum's literature mentions salt aerosol has "long been used" for decongestant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-microbial properties. Key Features — What They Are and Why They Matter

Climate Equalizer (3-Speed, Whisper-Quiet): This is the heart of the Saunum difference. The fan module runs at 3 speeds and is described as "whisper-quiet" — important because nobody wants a noisy fan in their relaxation space. It draws hot air from the ceiling through the telescopic intake, blends it with cool floor air pulled through the side-mounted valve, and pushes the mixed air out through the front grille.

How to Frame This:

The core concept: Think of the Climate Equalizer like a convection oven vs. a regular oven. A regular oven has hot spots and cold spots. A convection oven uses a fan to circulate air evenly. The food (you!) cooks more evenly, more comfortably, and more efficiently. Same principle, applied to sauna.

What the customer needs to understand: This isn't just comfort — it's efficiency. Even heat distribution means the heater doesn't have to work as hard to make the sauna feel right. And you'll stay in longer because you're not fighting discomfort.

Himalayan Salt Reservoir (Removable, Slotted Tray — 16.5" × 4.5"): Seven Himalayan salt spheres sit in a slotted tray positioned directly in the airstream. As the Climate Equalizer moves air through the heater body, it passes over these salt spheres, picking up salt ions and distributing them throughout the room. This creates a functional salt sauna experience — not decorative salt panels on the wall, but active ion distribution.

Self-Drying Function: After your sauna session, the fan system can run independently to circulate air and help dry out the sauna room. Moisture is the enemy of sauna wood — this feature extends the life of the entire sauna build, not just the heater.

Incoloy Heating Elements with Air Tunnel Protection: Three slotted stainless steel air tunnels surround the heating elements, protecting them from direct water contact while still allowing heat to transfer efficiently to the stones. This extends element life significantly.

UL Certification: Every Saunum heater is UL listed. This matters because many municipalities require UL-listed equipment for permits and inspections. It also matters for insurance — a non-UL heater can create issues with homeowner's insurance claims.

A well-ventilated sauna is essential for comfort and safety. Saunum's air blending enhances what good ventilation starts — it does not replace it. Always recommend customers plan ventilation with their builder.

VentLocationSizeDuring UseAfter Use
Fresh Air IntakeLow on wall, near heater, same side as heater air inletStandardOpenOpen

DryingOpposite wall, near ceiling or in ceilingStandardCLOSEDOpen (to dry out moisture)

Watch Out: The drying vent must stay CLOSED while the sauna is in use. An open drying vent disrupts airflow and prevents the room from reaching the desired temperature. Only open it after your sauna session is finished, so moist air can escape and the sauna room stays healthy and free of mold/mildew.

When a customer asks about sauna ventilation:

"Ventilation is one of the most important parts of a sauna that people overlook. You need three vents: a fresh air intake low on the wall near the heater, an exhaust vent on the opposite wall under the bench, and a drying vent near the ceiling. The drying vent stays closed while you're using the sauna — you only open it afterward to let the moisture escape. If you're pairing this with a Saunum heater, the air blending device works with these vents to actively circulate and mix the air for an even more comfortable climate. I'd definitely recommend consulting the heater manual for exact sizing."
