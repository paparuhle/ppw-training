Chapter 2: Understanding Wood-Burning Saunas (Foundations)

Before we dive into the specific Cozy Heat models, you need to understand how wood-burning sauna stoves work. If you've only ever sold electric heaters, this chapter gets you up to speed.

Key Terms You Need to Know

Key Definition — Kilowatt (kW): A measurement of heat output. Think of it like horsepower for a car engine — the higher the kW, the more heat the stove can produce. Cozy Heat stoves come in 12kW and 18kW options. A 12kW stove is for smaller rooms; 18kW handles larger spaces.

Key Definition — Firebox: The chamber inside the stove where you burn the wood. It's like the engine compartment. The size of the firebox determines how much wood you can load at once, which affects how long you can run between loadings.

Key Definition — Flame Cutter: A component inside the firebox that directs and distributes the flame. It takes the most heat abuse of any part. In Cozy Heat stoves, this is made of 5mm-thick stainless steel — significantly thicker than most competitors. Think of it as the brake pads of the stove: it's the wear component, and Cozy builds it tougher.

Key Definition — Dual Combustion (Secondary Burn): Regular wood stoves burn wood once and the smoke goes up the chimney. Dual combustion stoves burn the smoke gases a second time — extracting more heat from the same amount of wood and producing dramatically cleaner exhaust. This is why Cozy Heat stoves are 6x cleaner and use 25-40% less fuel.

Key Definition — Flue / Chimney Connection: The pipe that carries smoke and exhaust gases out of the sauna. All Cozy Heat stoves have a top-mounted flue connection, meaning the chimney pipe attaches to the top of the stove and goes straight up through the roof.

Key Definition — Thru-Wall (TW): A stove design where the firebox extends through the sauna wall so you can load firewood from outside the sauna room. The heat and stones stay inside; the door where you add wood is on the other side of the wall. This keeps the sauna cleaner (no bark, no ash inside) and means you don't lose heat every time you open the door to add wood.

Key Definition — BImSchV Certification: Germany's federal emissions standard for wood-burning appliances. It's the strictest in Europe — like getting a 5-star crash rating for emissions. Cozy Heat stoves meet EN 15821:2010, BImSchV DIN EN 13240, Level 2, the highest certification level. This is a big deal for customers in areas with emissions regulations or for anyone who cares about clean burning.

Key Definition — Sauna Stones: Natural rocks placed on top of the stove that absorb and store heat. You pour water (called löyly — pronounced "LOW-loo") over the hot stones to create steam. The stones act like a thermal battery — they absorb heat while the fire is burning and release it slowly, providing that mild, long-lasting heat wood saunas are famous for. More stone mass = more stored heat = longer, more stable sauna sessions.

How a Wood-Burning Sauna Stove Works

Here's the simple version:

  1. You load firewood into the firebox and light it. Cozy Heat 18kW stoves accept logs up to 15.74" (400mm) long; 12kW models accept up to 12.99" (329mm). [verify against source — not confirmed in parsed specs] Firewood diameter: 3-5".
  2. The fire heats the stove body, which radiates heat into the room. The dual combustion system burns the smoke gases a second time for maximum efficiency.
  3. The stones on top absorb heat from the stove body. With 175-350 lbs of stones (depending on the model), you have a massive thermal battery.
  4. You pour water over the hot stones to create steam (löyly). This is what gives a traditional sauna its characteristic soft, enveloping heat.
  5. Exhaust gases exit through the top-mounted flue and up the chimney. Thanks to dual combustion, these gases are 6x cleaner than conventional stoves.

How to Frame This:

The core concept: Wood-fired saunas are the original, the OG. Electric heaters are the modern convenience version. Think of it like grilling — a gas grill is convenient, but a charcoal grill gives you that authentic flavor and experience. Wood-fired saunas produce a softer, more radiant heat that sauna purists swear by.

What the customer needs to understand: A wood-fired sauna isn't harder — it's more intentional. The ritual of building the fire IS part of the experience. And with Cozy Heat's dual combustion, you get the traditional experience without the downsides (excess smoke, high wood consumption).

Understanding Stone Mass

Stone mass is one of the most important factors in a wood-burning sauna, and it's something your customer probably hasn't thought about.

Think of sauna stones as a thermal battery. The more stones you have, the more heat energy is stored. More stored energy means:

  • Longer sessions without needing to add more wood
  • More stable temperatures — less fluctuation
  • Better steam (löyly) — more hot stone surface area for water to hit

Cozy Heat stoves require between 175 and 350 lbs of stones depending on the model and kW rating. That's a LOT of thermal mass — which translates to an exceptional sauna experience.

Why stone mass is a feature, not a burden: Heat stratification is the enemy of a great sauna. Hot air rises, so cheap stoves with minimal stones create an uncomfortable split — your head cooks while your feet freeze. That's why people lie down on sauna benches: not because they're tired, but because they want their whole body at the same temperature. A heavy stone mass (like Cozy Heat's 175-350 lbs) radiates heat from a lower point, creating a more even temperature profile throughout the room. The result? You can stay in the sauna for the 15-20 minutes that research shows you need for real health benefits — cardiovascular, muscle recovery, stress reduction — instead of being chased out after 8 minutes because your head is overheating.

Why This Matters for Sales:

When a customer says "that seems like a lot of stones," flip it: "That's the whole point. More stones means softer, more even heat that lets you actually stay in the sauna long enough to get the health benefits. Cheap stoves with small stone beds blast your head with heat and your feet stay cold. That's not a sauna — that's a hot box." The stone mass is the selling point, not the objection.

Watch Out: Stones are a required component sold separately from the stove. Always confirm the customer is aware they need to purchase stones. Cozy Heat offers two stone options: Traditional Sauna Stones (10-15 cm, natural shape) and Premium Rounded Olivine Diabase Stones (smooth, river-stone look). Recommend buying an extra box to allow for selection of the best-fitting stones.

Chimney & Ventilation Basics

Every wood-burning stove needs a chimney. All Cozy Heat models have a top-mounted flue connection, meaning the chimney pipe goes straight up from the top of the stove. Here's what you need to tell customers:

  • A proper chimney is required — this is typically not included and must be sourced locally based on local building codes
  • The sauna room needs adequate ventilation for combustion air (the fire needs oxygen)
  • Professional installation is strongly recommended
  • Local building codes and fire regulations must be followed

Watch Out: Never tell a customer they can install a wood-burning stove without checking local building codes. Many municipalities have specific requirements for wood-burning appliances, chimney heights, clearances, and permits. Always recommend they consult with a local professional.

Ventilation Quick Reference

Proper ventilation is critical for both combustion and sauna comfort. A well-ventilated wood sauna uses a three-vent system:

Understanding wood-burning fundamentals — how the fire works, why stones matter, how ventilation flows — makes you the expert in the room. Most customers have never owned a wood-burning stove.
