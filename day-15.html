HBOT Health, Customers & Sessions

Estimated time: ~65 minutes · Source: Hyperbaric_Chamber_Product_Type_Guide.html
Day 15 shifts from hardware to humans. Who buys these chambers, what conditions they're actually hoping to address, what a session actually looks like, and where they're going to put the thing. Strong day for customer discovery fluency.

What you'll learn today

  • The research landscape: what's strong evidence, what's promising, what's anecdotal.
  • Seven customer profiles (biohacker, athlete, chronic condition manager, post-surgical, aging parent, wellness practitioner, TBI/concussion).
  • What a full session looks like: pressurization, treatment, depressurization, post-session.
  • Room requirements: size, floor load, electrical, ventilation, noise, temperature.
  • Apartment vs. house considerations and the space planning checklist.

Why it matters

Customer profile matching is what turns 'shopping' into 'buying.' If you can identify the profile in the first five minutes, you can tailor the rest of the call to that story.
