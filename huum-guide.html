Chapter 1: Introduction to HUUM

What Is HUUM?

HUUM is an Estonian sauna heater manufacturer that designs, engineers, and builds every product in Estonia — a country where sauna culture isn't a hobby, it's a way of life. Estonia has roughly 1.3 million people and an estimated 1+ million saunas — nearly one for every person in the country. Sauna is woven into daily life there the way morning coffee is here. HUUM was born from that culture, and their design philosophy reflects it: the heater should be beautiful enough to be the centerpiece of the room, powerful enough to produce authentic löyly (the Finnish/Estonian word for the steam that rises from sauna stones), and engineered to last in the extreme environment of a sauna. That combination of design ambition + cultural authenticity + engineering rigor is what makes HUUM different from brands that just happen to make heaters.

HUUM's lineup spans electric heaters (wall-mount and floor-standing) and wood-burning stoves, covering saunas from tiny 106 cu. ft. home builds all the way up to 989 cu. ft. commercial installations.

Why Do We Carry HUUM?

HUUM fills a critical gap in our lineup. They sit between our entry-level brand (Harvia) and our ultra-premium brand (Saunum). HUUM gives customers who care about design and aesthetics a step up from the utilitarian look of most heaters — without jumping to the price point of Saunum.

How to Frame This:

The core concept: The car analogy. If Harvia is Toyota — reliable, gets the job done, great value — then HUUM is BMW or Mercedes. You're paying for superior design, better materials, and a more refined experience. It's for the customer who wants their sauna to look as good as it feels. And if they ask about the absolute top of the market, Saunum is the Bentley — air blending technology, salt therapy, UL listed. HUUM sits in the premium-but-attainable BMW lane: stunning design without the Bentley price tag.

What the customer needs to understand: HUUM heaters are designed to be a visual centerpiece, not just functional hardware. Products like the DROP (Red Dot Award winner) and the HIVE (iconic beehive shape) turn the heater into a statement piece.

PPW framework to apply: Three Tens — build certainty in the Product. HUUM's design pedigree and Estonian craftsmanship are the proof points.

  • Design-forward: The DROP won the prestigious European Red Dot Award. The HIVE's beehive shape is instantly recognizable. The CORE can be embedded into a bench and customized with wood, tile, or stone. No other brand in our lineup offers this level of aesthetic flexibility.
  • Massive stone capacity for their size: HUUM heaters consistently hold more stone than competitors of the same kW rating. More stone = softer, longer-lasting heat and better steam.

Chapter 2: Understanding Sauna Heaters (Foundations)

Before we dive into specific HUUM products, let's make sure you understand the fundamentals. If you already know this material, skip ahead to Chapter 3. But if you've never sold a sauna heater before, read this carefully — it's the foundation everything else builds on.

Key Terms You Need to Know

Key Definition — Kilowatt (kW): A unit of power that measures how much heat a heater produces. Think of it like horsepower in a car. A 6kW heater is like a 4-cylinder engine — fine for a small car (small sauna). An 18kW heater is like a V8 — built for a bigger vehicle (large or commercial sauna). More kW = more heating power = can heat a bigger room.

Key Definition — Cubic Feet (cu. ft.): The volume of the sauna room, calculated as Length × Width × Height (all in feet). A 6' × 6' × 7' sauna = 252 cu. ft. This is the number you match to a heater's rated capacity.

Key Definition — 240V / Single Phase (1PH): The electrical supply the heater needs. 240V is the same type of circuit your clothes dryer or electric oven runs on — it's a dedicated, higher-voltage circuit. "Single phase" means standard residential power (as opposed to three-phase commercial power). All HUUM electric heaters operate on 240V/1PH, which is great news: your customer's home already has this type of power available.

Key Definition — Amperage (Amps): How much electrical current the heater draws. Higher kW heaters draw more amps and need thicker wiring and larger circuit breakers. This determines the electrical work the customer's electrician needs to do.

Key Definition — AWG (American Wire Gauge): The standard for wire thickness. Lower numbers = thicker wire. A 10 AWG wire handles smaller heaters; 6 AWG handles larger ones. The electrician needs to know this.

Key Definition — Stone Capacity: How many pounds of sauna stones the heater holds. This is a critical performance indicator. Think of stones as a thermal battery — they absorb heat, store it, and release it slowly and evenly. More stone mass = softer, more consistent heat and better steam when you pour water (called "löyly" in Finnish/Estonian sauna culture).

Key Definition — Safety Distances: The minimum clearance required between the heater and combustible surfaces (walls, benches, ceilings). These are non-negotiable — they're set by the manufacturer and by building codes. Smaller safety distances mean the heater can fit in tighter spaces.

Key Definition — LS (Lungo Sportello / Firebox Extension): On wood-burning HUUM stoves, "LS" means the firebox is extended so it can poke through the wall. This lets you load firewood from outside the sauna room (like a changing room or hallway) instead of opening the door and letting heat escape.

Wall-Mount vs. Floor-Standing

Wall-mount heaters (DROP, CLIFF Mini) hang on the wall, saving floor space. They're ideal for small saunas where every square inch matters.

Floor-standing heaters (HIVE, HIVE Mini, STEEL, CLIFF 6/9/11, CORE) sit on the sauna floor. They typically hold more stones and are better for medium-to-large rooms.

Pro Tip: Note that the CLIFF 6, 9, and 11 are listed as "floor models" in the source data despite the CLIFF family sometimes being associated with wall mounting. The CLIFF Mini 4 is specifically a wall-mount model. Always confirm mounting type from the spec sheet.

How Stone Capacity Affects the Experience

This is one of the most important concepts you'll explain to customers. Consider this: Prit from Bathing Brands — who is Estonian, from a country where everyone has a sauna — admitted on our training call that he didn't enjoy saunas for years because of heat stratification and poor heater experiences. If someone from the sauna capital of the world can be put off by an inadequate heater, it underscores just how much the heater choice matters. The right stone capacity is a huge part of that equation. Here's the analogy:

How to Frame This:

The core concept: Stones are a thermal battery. A heater with 66 lbs of stone is like a small battery pack — it heats up fast but doesn't store as much energy. A heater with 528 lbs of stone is like a massive battery bank — it takes longer to charge but delivers smooth, consistent power for hours.

What the customer needs to understand: More stones = softer heat (less "prickly"), longer sessions without temperature drops, and better steam when you pour water. HUUM heaters are designed to maximize stone-to-element contact, which means even their smaller heaters punch above their weight.

Why the Heater Is the Most Important Purchase

This is a foundational concept from Bathing Brands training. Customers routinely spend $5,000–$7,000+ on a sauna cabin but then try to save money on the heater. That's backwards — the heater is the sauna experience.

How to Frame — The Heater Upgrade Conversation:

When a customer hesitates on the heater budget:

Lead with the industry data point: according to Harvia's quarterly earnings report, 70 to 80 percent of heater sales go to second-time buyers, and they almost always upgrade to a better, more expensive model. The implication is clear — most people regret cheaping out on the heater the first time around.

Reinforce with the First Car Analogy:

Compare the heater purchase to buying a car. The first time, people are just excited to have one and don't think about features. By the second purchase, they care about comfort, quality, and longevity. Saunas follow the same pattern — position the better heater as skipping the costly do-over.

Pro Tip — The Saunum Air Solo Upsell: If a customer has already committed to a HUUM heater but you think they'd benefit from air blending technology, Saunum sells the Air Solo as a standalone wall-mount device. It works with any heater — including HUUM — and transforms the sauna experience by evening out temperature from floor to ceiling. This is a great add-on sale that doesn't require the customer to change their heater choice.

CLIFF Family — The Space Saver

Electric | Wall & Floor Mount | 4–11 kW

The CLIFF is HUUM's answer to tight spaces. It's a slim, tower-style heater with remarkably small safety distances — as little as 6 cm (about 2.4") from combustible walls on smaller models, and 12 cm (~4.7") on larger ones. That means it can tuck into corners that would be off-limits for bulkier heaters.

Understanding the variants: The number after "CLIFF" is the kW rating (6, 9, 11). Higher kW = more heating power for bigger rooms. The CLIFF Mini 4 is a compact 4kW version that mounts on the wall — it's the only wall-mount in the family and is purpose-built for very small saunas (106–177 cu. ft.).

Key Features — What They Are and Why They Matter

  • Internal air duct: A channel inside the heater body that pre-heats incoming air. This means the CLIFF heats up to 25% faster than heaters without this feature. Why it matters to the customer: Less waiting. Frame this as "you get in your sauna faster, and your electricity bill is lower because it's not running as long to reach temperature."
  • 165 lb stone capacity (132 lbs on Mini): Impressive for a heater this slim. All those stones sit in direct contact with the heating elements. Frame it: "It's like a sports car with a surprisingly big engine — it doesn't look like it should hold this much stone, but it does."
  • Removable ribs: The vertical slats on the heater body can be removed to allow more precise stone stacking. The CLIFF ships with 2 prop blocks specifically for removing the rib slats. This is a small but thoughtful design detail that shows HUUM's attention to the user experience.
  • Stainless steel construction: Industrial-quality elements and body. Built to handle the extreme moisture and temperature cycles of a sauna.

CORE Family — The Customizable Chameleon

Electric | Floor Mount | 6.5–10.5 kW | Embeddable in bench

The CORE is HUUM's most versatile heater from a design perspective. It can be completely embedded into a sauna bench, making it nearly invisible. It comes in 5 exterior finish options, and the BK/ST version ships without panels so customers can customize with their own tile, stone, glass, or wood.

Understanding the variants: Unlike other families where the number = kW, CORE variants differ by exterior material/finish. All CORE variants are available in 6.5kW, 9kW, or 10.5kW (selected at time of order). The five finishes are:

Key Features

  • Bench-embeddable: The CORE can sit inside a bench cutout so only the stone surface is visible from above. This is unique in our lineup and a huge design selling point.
